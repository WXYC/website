
# FAQ (From old page)

1\. What is WXDU?

WXDU, as a member of the Duke University Union, exists to inform,
educate, and entertain both the students of Duke University and the
surrounding community of Durham through quality progressive alternative
radio programming. WXDU seeks to give its staff the freedom to pursue
their personal aesthetic within the framework of a cohesive format. WXDU
aims to provide the listener with an alternative viewpoint untainted by
commercial interests. WXDU resolves to maintain good relations with the
music industry without compromising its integrity and nationally
recognized commitment to quality programming. WXDU resolves to remain a
laboratory where all members are free to make and learn from their
mistakes.

2.  How can I listen to WXDU?
You can listen to WXDU on your FM radio if you tune into 88.7.  The
station is on Duke's East Campus, but the transmitter and tower are out
in the Duke forest closer to Hillsborough.  You can usually pick the
signal up pretty well in Durham and slightly to the east.  Weather
patterns can sometimes affect clarity.  You can also listen to WXDU
programming via the internet.  You can listen by clicking on the "Listen
Now" link in the top right corner of this page.  We stream our live
broadcast in 2 main formats, MP3 (mainly for iTunes users, but you can listen on many other media players) and Ogg Vorbis (read about Ogg Vorbis files HERE).  
Decide which format you prefer and click it to open it in your chosen
media player.  WXDU also has a few programs that are available as free
podcasts via iTunesU.  You can check those shows out on the Podcasts page of directly in the iTunes store by searching for WXDU.

4\. Can you air a public service announcement for me/my organization?

We’ll be happy to assist in raising awareness of events and issues
for your organization.  Keep in mind that we are a non-commercial
educational radio station, so we can’t advertise, but we can definitely
give information about events and issues that benefit our community. 
Please email [psa@wxdu.org](mailto\:psa@wxdu.org) with all your information.  You can also mail us information about your
group/event.  Send it via snail mail to WXDU PSA Director, PO Box
90689, Durham, NC 27708.  Be sure to include dates, times, locations,
event descriptions, contact information, and any other relevant
material.  Please allow some time for us to get your PSA up on the air.

6\. How can I get my music on the air?

Send us your music.  If its good, we’ll play it. 
Mail it to us at WXDU Music Director, PO Box 90689, Durham NC 27708. 
Once we receive your music our Music Staff will listen to your record
and if we like it, we'll play it.  Please allow us some time to review
it before you start calling our Music Directors.  You can reach them at
919-684-2957.  Please don't call the request line.  Please feel free to
include info with your music submission (i.e. track lists, album
artwork, etc), but don't worry about sending a press kit.  Its not
needed.  If you are touring, let us know well in advance, so we can try
to help you out.  Please keep in mind that we can't (and don't want to)
play everything.

 
7\. How can I become a DJ for WXDU?

Training groups for new DJs are formed 3 times a year, during the
Spring, Summer and Fall. Training takes about 6 weeks. You do not have
to be a Duke student or have any experience to train-- just a love of
many kinds of non-commercial music! If you'd like to be included in our
next training group, contact our training director at [training@wxdu.org](mailto\:training@wxdu.org)

8\. Are there other ways to get involved at WXDU?

We’re always looking for volunteers to pitch in here at WXDU. 
Becoming a DJ is just one way to get involved.  If you are interested in
learning more about music and also helping us select the music that
goes on the air, then you might want to come join Music Staff.  They
meet at the station every Sunday at 6pm.  You can contact our Music
Directors at [music@wxdu.org](mailto\:music@wxdu.org). 
If you are interested in promotions, station operations, news/talk
programming, or public affairs you can join our Community Affairs
Committee.  They meet every other Monday at 6:30pm at the station.  You
can contact our Community Affairs Director at [promotions@wxdu.org](mailto\:promotions@wxdu.org).  If you’re not sure where you might fit in, just email our General Manager at [gm@wxdu.org](mailto\:gm@wxdu.org), and they’ll be happy to talk with you.

11.  Can my band play live or do an interview at WXDU?

We occaisionaly host live bands for performances or interviews at the
station.  Most of these are local bands as part of the Local Music Show
